# Construction N1-CON-003: Marker-Three Valuation Rainbow

Result label: **heuristic** as a complete half-range route.

Its legality, numerical distinctness, exact support lattice, quotient correction reduction, and unconditional initial interval are proved theorems. Uniform half-range occupancy remains open.

## 1. Reason for the revision

The preceding preferred route `N1-CON-001` was disproved by Nova 2 at:

- branch: `nova/additive-occupancy`

Every old main term was divisible by `2^(r_n+1)`, while the correction radius was `2^r_n-1`. The first required target was therefore inaccessible.

This version places the fixed marker `3` in every main term, starts the 2-adic addresses at zero, and uses pure powers of two only in the correction palette.

## 2. Formal definition

For every integer `n>=3`, set

\[
X_n=\lfloor\sqrt{n!}\rfloor,
\qquad
r_n=\lceil4\log n\rceil,
\qquad
M_n=\lceil16(\log n)^2\rceil,
\]

\[
R_n=2^{r_n}-1,
\qquad
W_n=\left\lfloor\frac{R_n-2}{3}\right\rfloor.
\]

For `1<=t<=M_n`, define

\[
U_t(n)=
\left\{
 u\ge1:
 u\text{ odd},\ 3u\mid n!,\ 3\cdot2^{t-1}u\le X_n
\right\},
\]

and

\[
A_t^{(3)}(n)=
\{3\cdot2^{t-1}u:u\in U_t(n)\}.
\]

A legal main selection chooses

\[
d_t\in A_t^{(3)}(n)\cup\{0\}
\]

for each layer and forms

\[
y=\sum_{t=1}^{M_n}d_t.
\]

The correction palette is

\[
C_n=\{1,2,4,\ldots,2^{r_n-1}\}.
\]

The associated quotient layer is

\[
B_t(n)=
\{2^{t-1}u:u\in U_t(n)\}.
\]

## 3. Legal divisor proof

Result label: **proved theorem**.

For all sufficiently large `n`,

\[
M_n-1\le v_2(n!).
\]

For every `u in U_t(n)`, the factor `3u` divides `n!`, `u` is odd, and the address exponent `t-1` does not exceed the available 2-adic valuation. Hence

\[
3\cdot2^{t-1}u\mid n!.
\]

The numerical upper cutoff is part of the menu definition, so every selected main divisor is at most `X_n`.

Every correction power of two also divides `n!` for sufficiently large `n` because `r_n-1<=v_2(n!)`.

## 4. Numerical distinctness proof

Result label: **proved theorem**.

Within one layer, different cores produce different values.

Across two layers `s ne t`, every nonzero term has exact 2-adic valuation

\[
v_2(d_t)=t-1,
\]

so equality is impossible.

Every main term is divisible by `3`, whereas no correction term is divisible by `3`. Therefore no main divisor can collide with the binary palette.

The proof permits the same odd core to appear in multiple layers because the 2-adic addresses remain different.

## 5. Exact lattice and residue audit

Result label: **proved theorem**.

Every main term is in `3Z`, and the first layer contains `3` through the core `u=1`. Thus the exact additive subgroup generated by the main support is

\[
3\mathbb Z.
\]

The binary palette contains `1` and `2`, so its subset sums attain every residue modulo `3`. The combined construction therefore has no inaccessible residue class modulo its main support lattice.

This directly repairs the obstruction `N2-ADD-115`.

## 6. Exact quotient reduction

Write every target as

\[
x=3q+\delta,
\qquad
\delta\in\{0,1,2\}.
\]

If `q<=W_n`, then

\[
x\le3W_n+2\le R_n,
\]

and the binary palette handles the target alone.

For larger `q`, it is sufficient to find a quotient rainbow sum

\[
s=\sum_{t=1}^{M_n}b_t,
\qquad
b_t\in B_t(n)\cup\{0\},
\]

with

\[
q-W_n\le s\le q.
\]

Then the main sum `3s` lies below `x`, and the residual is

\[
x-3s=\delta+3(q-s)\le2+3W_n\le R_n.
\]

The palette repairs the residual.

Therefore the exact missing additive theorem is a downward-window theorem on the quotient layers with radius `W_n`, not an exact representation theorem and not the false old lattice contract.

## 7. Selectable term budget

Result label: **proved theorem**.

At most one main term is selected from each of `M_n` layers, and at most `r_n` correction terms are used. Thus

\[
K_n=M_n+r_n
=\lceil16(\log n)^2\rceil+\lceil4\log n\rceil.
\]

No stagewise or recursive multiplier occurs.

## 8. Unconditional initial interval

Result label: **proved theorem**.

Let `m_n` be the largest odd integer at most `n`. For all sufficiently large `n`, every odd integer `u<=m_n` belongs to every core menu. This follows because `3u|n!` and

\[
3\cdot2^{M_n-1}m_n\le X_n.
\]

The quotient layers therefore contain the digit families

\[
2^{t-1}\{1,3,5,\ldots,m_n\}.
\]

The odd-digit one-gap lemma proves downward one-density through

\[
m_n(2^{M_n}-1).
\]

Consequently every integer

\[
0\le x\le3m_n(2^{M_n}-1)+2
\]

has a legal representation using at most `M_n+r_n` distinct divisors of `n!`.

This is an unconditional asymptotic structural theorem, but its range is only `exp(O((log n)^2))`, far below the factorial half-range.

## 9. Menu capacity

Every prime in `(n/2,n]` occurs to exponent one in `n!`, is odd for sufficiently large `n`, and can be used in an odd core while the marker `3` is reserved separately.

As in the earlier high-prime menu lemma, complementary subset products show that each layer has exponentially many legal high-prime cores once

\[
3\cdot2^{M_n-1}\sqrt{\prod_{n/2<p\le n}p}\le X_n.
\]

This side condition holds for all sufficiently large `n` because the logarithm of the left side is `O(n+(log n)^2)`, while `log X_n=Theta(n log n)`.

This proves profile capacity only. It does not prove additive occupancy.

## 10. Collision analysis

- Within-layer divisor collision: impossible because cores are numerical values.
- Cross-layer divisor collision: impossible by exact 2-adic valuation.
- Main-palette collision: impossible by divisibility by `3`.
- Duplicate total sums: possible and still an active issue.
- Quotient profile collision: possible and must be handled by Nova 2 or falsified by Nova 4.
- Hidden prime dependence: present because different cores may share prime factors, but additive legality is termwise and does not multiply selected terms.

## 11. Correction mechanism

\[

The correction radius exceeds the largest unresolved residue gap modulo the main lattice `3Z`, which is `2`.

## 15. Mandatory falsification duties

The route must be rejected if any of the following occurs:

1. a main term fails to divide `n!`;
2. two selected layer terms can be numerically equal;
3. a main term can collide with a correction power of two;
4. the quotient layers have a persistent residue obstruction not repaired by `W_n`;
5. the quotient rainbow set has a downward gap larger than `W_n`;
6. the endpoint `floor(X_n/3)` is outside the attainable support;
7. effective profile collisions reduce the support below the counting threshold;
8. a proof introduces sequential interval extension and falls under a Phase 12P-type obstruction;
9. finite success is promoted to an asymptotic theorem.
