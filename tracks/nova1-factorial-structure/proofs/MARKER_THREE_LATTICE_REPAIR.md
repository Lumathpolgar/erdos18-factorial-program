The rejected Nova 1 construction used addresses `e_t=r_n+t`, so every main sum was divisible by `2^(r_n+1)` while the correction radius was only `2^r_n-1`.

The repair below changes both the marker and the address origin. It does not claim the factorial half-range theorem.

## Frozen parameters

For an integer `n>=3`, define

\[
X_n=\lfloor\sqrt{n!}\rfloor,
\qquad
r_n=\lceil4\log n\rceil,
\qquad
M_n=\lceil16(\log n)^2\rceil,
\]

and

\[
R_n=2^{r_n}-1,
\qquad
W_n=\left\lfloor\frac{R_n-2}{3}\right\rfloor.
\]

For `1<=t<=M_n`, define the odd-core menu

\[
U_t(n)=
\left\{
 u\in\mathbb Z_{>0}:
 u\text{ is odd},\ 3u\mid n!,\ 3\cdot2^{t-1}u\le X_n
\right\}.
\]

Define the main layer

\[
A_t^{(3)}(n)=
\left\{
3\cdot2^{t-1}u:u\in U_t(n)
\right\}.
\]

Define the quotient layer

\[
B_t(n)=
\left\{
2^{t-1}u:u\in U_t(n)
\right\}.
\]

The correction palette is

\[
C_n=\{1,2,4,\ldots,2^{r_n-1}\}.
\]

A rainbow selection chooses at most one term from each main layer.

## N1-STR-014: legality and numerical distinctness

Result label: **proved theorem**.

Assume

\[
M_n-1\le v_2(n!).
\]

Then every member of every `A_t^(3)(n)` is a positive divisor of `n!`.

### Proof

Let

\[
d=3\cdot2^{t-1}u\in A_t^{(3)}(n).
\]

By definition, `3u|n!`, `u` is odd, and `t-1<=v_2(n!)`. The factor `3u` consumes no factor of `2`, so the prime valuations of `d` do not exceed those of `n!`. Hence `d|n!`.

Within one layer, different cores give different numerical divisors. Across different layers,

\[
v_2(d)=t-1,
\]

so terms from different layers are numerically distinct. Every main term is divisible by `3`, while no correction term is divisible by `3`. Thus the main layers and correction palette are numerically disjoint. `QED`

## N1-STR-015: exact support lattice and residues

Result label: **proved theorem**.

For all sufficiently large `n` satisfying `3<=X_n`, the additive subgroup generated by all main-layer terms is exactly

\[
3\mathbb Z.
\]

The combined main-plus-palette system attains every residue class modulo `3`.

### Proof

Every main term is divisible by `3`, so the generated subgroup is contained in `3Z`.

The first layer contains the term `3`, because `u=1` is legal when `3<=X_n`. Therefore the generated subgroup contains `3`, and it is exactly `3Z`.

The correction palette contains `1` and `2` once `r_n>=2`. Its subset sums attain residues `0`, `1`, and `2` modulo `3`. Adding a main sum does not change the residue modulo `3`, so every residue class is attained. `QED`

## N1-RED-004: quotient-window correction theorem

Result label: **proved theorem**.

Assume the legality side condition above. Suppose that for every integer

\[
W_n+1\le q\le\left\lfloor\frac{X_n}{3}\right\rfloor
\]

there is a quotient rainbow sum

\[
s=\sum_{t=1}^{M_n}b_t,
\qquad
b_t\in B_t(n)\cup\{0\},
\]

such that

\[
q-W_n\le s\le q.
\]

Then every integer

\[
0\le x\le X_n
\]

is a sum of at most

\[
M_n+r_n
\]

distinct positive divisors of `n!`.

Consequently,

\[
H_{n!}(X_n+1)\le M_n+r_n=O((\log n)^2).
\]

### Proof

Write

\[
x=3q+\delta,
\qquad
q=\left\lfloor\frac{x}{3}\right\rfloor,
\qquad
\delta\in\{0,1,2\}.
\]

If `q<=W_n`, then

\[
x\le3W_n+2\le R_n,
\]

so the binary palette represents `x` directly using at most `r_n` terms.

Now assume `q>=W_n+1`. Choose a quotient rainbow sum `s` with

\[
q-W_n\le s\le q.
\]

Multiplying every selected quotient term by `3` gives a legal main rainbow sum

\[
y=3s.
\]

The residual is

\[
x-y=\delta+3(q-s).
\]

It is nonnegative and satisfies

\[
x-y\le2+3W_n\le R_n.
\]

The binary palette represents the residual. Main terms and palette terms are numerically disjoint by N1-STR-014. The total number of selected terms is at most `M_n+r_n`. `QED`

## N1-STR-016: odd-digit one-gap lemma

Result label: **proved theorem**.

Let `m>=3` be odd and let

\[
D_m=\{0,1,3,5,\ldots,m\}.
\]

For an integer `L>=1`, define

\[
S_L(m)=
\left\{
\sum_{e=0}^{L-1}2^e a_e:a_e\in D_m
\right\}.
\]

Then every integer

\[
0\le q\le m(2^L-1)
\]

has some `s in S_L(m)` satisfying

\[
q-1\le s\le q.
\]

Equivalently, the maximum downward gap of `S_L(m)` on its full convex hull is at most `1`.

### Proof

For `L=1`, every odd integer in `[1,m]` belongs to `S_1(m)`. Every positive even integer `q<=m` has `q-1` odd, so the assertion holds.

Assume the assertion for `L`, and set

\[
A_L=m(2^L-1).
\]

The set `S_(L+1)(m)` is the union of translates

\[
2^L a+S_L(m),
\qquad a\in D_m.
\]

Each translate is downward one-dense on its interval

\[
[2^La,2^La+A_L].
\]

The first two digit values are `0` and `1`, whose translate starts differ by `2^L`. Since `A_L>=3(2^L-1)`, these two intervals overlap or leave a gap of length at most one.

Every later pair of consecutive nonzero digit values differs by `2`, so their translate starts differ by `2^(L+1)`. Again,

\[
A_L=m(2^L-1)\ge3(2^L-1)\ge2^{L+1}-2.
\]

Thus consecutive translated intervals overlap or leave at most one missing integer. Their union is downward one-dense from `0` through

\[
A_L+2^Lm=m(2^{L+1}-1).
\]

This completes the induction. `QED`

## N1-STR-017: unconditional initial interval

Result label: **proved theorem**.

Let `m_n` be the largest odd integer at most `n`. For all sufficiently large `n` satisfying

\[
3\cdot2^{M_n-1}m_n\le X_n
\]

and

\[
3u\mid n!\quad\text{for every odd }1\le u\le m_n,
\]

the marker-three construction represents every integer

\[
0\le x\le3m_n(2^{M_n}-1)+2
\]

using at most `M_n+r_n` distinct divisors of `n!`.

### Proof

Every odd `u<=m_n` belongs to every core menu `U_t(n)` under the displayed hypotheses. Therefore the quotient rainbow sumset contains `S_(M_n)(m_n)` from N1-STR-016.

For a target `x` in the stated interval, write `x=3q+delta` with `delta in {0,1,2}`. Then `q<=m_n(2^M_n-1)`. Choose `s` with `q-1<=s<=q`. The residual satisfies

\[
0\le x-3s\le5.
\]

For all sufficiently large `n`, `r_n>=3`, so `5<=R_n` and the binary palette represents the residual. Distinctness and legality follow from N1-STR-014. `QED`

The two displayed hypotheses hold for all sufficiently large `n`:

1. `v_3(n!)` eventually exceeds `1+floor(log_3 n)`, so `3u|n!` for every `u<=n`;
2. `M_n=O((log n)^2)` while `log X_n=Theta(n log n)`, so `3*2^(M_n-1)*n<=X_n` eventually.

## Exact term cost

Result label: **proved theorem**.

The full repaired architecture selects at most one main term from each of `M_n` layers and at most `r_n` binary correction terms. Its exact cost is

\[
\lceil16(\log n)^2\rceil+\lceil4\log n\rceil.
\]

No recursive multiplier is hidden in this count.

## What remains open

The unconditional initial interval is only

\[
\exp(O((\log n)^2)),
\]

while `X_n=exp(Theta(n log n))`. The route still requires a genuinely global additive theorem for the quotient layers.

The exact missing theorem is the hypothesis of N1-RED-004. Capacity, a common gcd of one, or finite one-gap behavior does not prove it.
