# Model F: Fourier and Local-Limit Occupancy

## Status

**Result label: conditional theorem.**

## Frozen setup

Let `Y_1,...,Y_k` be independent integer-valued label variables with legal, pairwise numerically disjoint nonzero supports. Set

\[
S=\sum_iY_i,
\qquad
\phi(t)=\mathbb E e^{itS}=\prod_i\mathbb E e^{itY_i}.
\]

Let `mu=E S`, `sigma^2=Var(S)`, and assume the additive subgroup generated by the support is `Z`.

For a target window `I=[x-R,x] cap Z`, define

\[
K_I(t)=\sum_{m\in I}e^{-imt}.
\]

Fourier inversion gives

\[
\mathbb P(S\in I)=
\frac{1}{2\pi}
\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}\phi(t)\overline{K_I(t)}\,dt.
\]

## Major and minor arcs

Fix `T>=1` and define

\[
\mathfrak M=\{t:|t|\le T/\sigma\},
\qquad
\mathfrak m=[-\pi,\pi]\setminus\mathfrak M.
\]

A valid theorem must give explicit bounds

\[
|\phi(t)-\psi(t)|\le E_{\rm maj}(t)
\quad(t\in\mathfrak M)
\]

for a lattice reference characteristic function `psi`, and

\[
\int_{\mathfrak m}
|\phi(t)-\psi(t)|\,|K_I(t)|\,dt
\le E_{\rm min}.
\]

The required positivity condition is

\[
\int_{\mathfrak M}E_{\rm maj}(t)|K_I(t)|\,dt
+E_{\rm min}
<2\pi Q(I),
\]

where `Q` is the reference lattice law with characteristic function `psi`.

## Lattice span

If

\[
g=\gcd\{s-s':s,s'\in\operatorname{supp}S\}>1,
\]

then `S` lies in one residue class modulo `g`. A local theorem on all consecutive integers is impossible. Span one, or a complete residue-correction theorem, is mandatory.

## Maximal-step condition

A typical major-arc expansion requires an explicit condition such as

\[
\rho:=rac{\max_i\|Y_i-\mathbb EY_i\|_\infty}{\sigma}\le\rho_0<1.
\]

No use of a central limit theorem is accepted unless the remainder is quantified in terms of `rho`, moments, and the exact target range.

## Window kernel

For `0<|t|<=pi`,

\[
|K_I(t)|\le\min(R+1,2/|t|).
\]

This is the mechanism by which a correction window can make a Fourier theorem easier than pointwise positivity.

## Endpoint regimes

A theorem centered at one fixed mean normally controls only a central range. If the law is tilted, the variance still needs a positive lower bound. Any endpoint region where `sigma` is bounded or vanishes must be excluded from the local-limit regime and covered separately.

## Required conclusion type

The only sufficient conclusions are:

- pointwise positivity for every target; or
- positive mass in every correction window.

An `o(1)` global distributional error, weak convergence, or an almost-all local theorem is insufficient unless its absolute error is smaller than the minimum target-window main term.
