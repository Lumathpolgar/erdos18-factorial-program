# Compact-Tilt Theorems for the Top Prime Band

## Scope and claim boundary

This file studies an exact factorial-divisor subfamily generated by primes in

\[
\mathcal P_n=\{p\text{ prime}:n/2<p\le n\}.
\]

Every `p in P_n` has `v_p(n!)=1`. Therefore its exponent is a Bernoulli coordinate and every subset product is a distinct divisor of `n!`.

The results concern logarithmic subset-product size. They do not imply additive occupancy for numerical divisor sums.

## N3-ANA-012, uniform compact-tilt Gaussian and coarse-window theorem

- Result class: `proved theorem`
- Repository status: `PROVED`
- External dependencies: N3-ANA-010 and the accepted Berry-Esseen theorem N3-SRC-003
- Effective constants: yes, in terms of any accepted universal Berry-Esseen constant `C_BE`
- Uniformity: all displayed `n`, `theta`, `x`, and `Delta`

### Definitions

Fix constants

\[
0\le\theta_0<1,
\qquad A\ge0.
\]

For every integer `n>=120368`, every real `theta` with `|theta|<=theta_0`, and every `p in P_n`, let `A_{p,theta}` be independent Bernoulli variables with

\[
\mathbb P_\theta(A_{p,\theta}=1)
=\frac{p^\theta}{1+p^\theta}.
\]

Define

\[
S_{n,\theta}=\sum_{p\in\mathcal P_n}A_{p,\theta}\log p,
\qquad
\mu_{n,\theta}=\mathbb E_\theta S_{n,\theta},
\]

\[
T_{n,\theta}=S_{n,\theta}-\mu_{n,\theta},
\qquad
B_{n,\theta}^2=\operatorname{Var}_\theta(T_{n,\theta}).
\]

Write

\[
v_{p,\theta}
=\operatorname{Var}_\theta(A_{p,\theta})
=\frac{p^\theta}{(1+p^\theta)^2}
=\frac{p^{-|\theta|}}{(1+p^{-|\theta|})^2}.
\]

### Variance and third-moment bounds

For every permitted `n` and `theta`,

\[
B_{n,\theta}^2
=\sum_{p\in\mathcal P_n}v_{p,\theta}(\log p)^2
\ge\frac1{48}n^{1-\theta_0}\log n.
\]

If

\[
X_{p,\theta}
=(A_{p,\theta}-\mathbb E_\theta A_{p,\theta})\log p,
\]

and

\[
\rho_{n,\theta}=\sum_{p\in\mathcal P_n}\mathbb E_\theta|X_{p,\theta}|^3,
\]

then

\[
\rho_{n,\theta}\le(\log n)B_{n,\theta}^2.
\]

Consequently Berry-Esseen gives the explicit uniform estimate

\[
\sup_z
\left|
\mathbb P_\theta\left\{\frac{T_{n,\theta}}{B_{n,\theta}}\le z\right\}
-\Phi(z)
\right|
\le
C_{BE}\frac{\log n}{B_{n,\theta}}
\le
C_{BE}\sqrt{\frac{48\log n}{n^{1-\theta_0}}}.
\]

In particular, uniformly over every sequence `|theta_n|<=theta_0`,

\[
\frac{T_{n,\theta_n}}{B_{n,\theta_n}}
\Rightarrow N(0,1).
\]

### Coarse-window positivity

Set

\[
c_A=\frac1{\sqrt{2\pi}}\exp\left(-\frac{(A+1)^2}{2}\right),
\qquad
K_A=\frac{4C_{BE}}{c_A}.
\]

For every real `x` and `Delta` satisfying

\[
|x|\le A B_{n,\theta},
\qquad
K_A\log n\le\Delta\le B_{n,\theta},
\]

we have

\[
\mathbb P_\theta\{T_{n,\theta}\in[x,x+\Delta]\}
\ge
\frac{c_A}{2}\frac{\Delta}{B_{n,\theta}}.
\]

Thus the admissible coarse-window width is `O_A(log n)`, uniformly over the compact tilt range `|theta|<=theta_0<1`.

### Exact weighted subset-product count

Let

\[
Z_{n,\theta}=\prod_{p\in\mathcal P_n}(1+p^\theta),
\]

and let `I=[a,b]` be the logarithmic interval

\[
a=\mu_{n,\theta}+x,
\qquad
b=a+\Delta.
\]

Define

\[
h_\theta(I)=\sup_{s\in I}\theta s
=
\begin{cases}
\theta b,&\theta\ge0,\\
\theta a,&\theta<0.
\end{cases}
\]

Then the number of distinct subset products in the interval satisfies

\[
\#\left\{
Q\subseteq\mathcal P_n:
\log\prod_{p\in Q}p\in I
\right\}
\ge
\frac{c_A}{2}\frac{\Delta}{B_{n,\theta}}
Z_{n,\theta}e^{-h_\theta(I)}.
\]

Every counted integer is a distinct divisor of `n!` by unique factorization.

### Proof

N3-ANA-010 gives

\[
m_n:=|\mathcal P_n|
=\pi(n)-\pi(n/2)
\ge\frac{n}{3\log n}
\]

for every `n>=120368`.

For `|theta|<=theta_0` and `p<=n`,

\[
v_{p,\theta}
=\frac{p^{-|\theta|}}{(1+p^{-|\theta|})^2}
\ge\frac14n^{-\theta_0}.
\]

Also `p>n/2` and `n>=4` imply

\[
\log p\ge\log(n/2)\ge\frac12\log n.
\]

Therefore

\[
B_{n,\theta}^2
\ge
\frac{n}{3\log n}\cdot
\frac14n^{-\theta_0}\cdot
\frac14(\log n)^2
=
\frac1{48}n^{1-\theta_0}\log n.
\]

Each centered coordinate satisfies `|X_{p,theta}|<=log p<=log n`. Hence

\[
\mathbb E_\theta|X_{p,\theta}|^3
\le
(\log n)\mathbb E_\theta X_{p,\theta}^2.
\]

Summing proves the third-moment bound. Berry-Esseen then gives the displayed Kolmogorov estimate.

For the window statement, standardize the interval. Its left endpoint lies in `[-A,A]` and its length is at most `1`. The standard normal density is therefore at least `c_A` throughout the standardized interval, so its Gaussian mass is at least

\[
c_A\frac{\Delta}{B_{n,\theta}}.
\]

The Berry-Esseen error enters at both endpoints. The hypothesis `Delta>=K_A log n` makes the total endpoint error at most half of the Gaussian lower bound. This proves coarse-window positivity.

For the counting statement, each subset `Q` has tilted probability

\[
\frac{\exp(\theta\log\prod_{p\in Q}p)}{Z_{n,\theta}}.
\]

Every subset product in `I` has probability at most `e^{h_theta(I)}/Z_{n,theta}`. Dividing the positive window probability by this maximal atom gives the count lower bound.

## N3-ANA-013, boundary obstruction at unit tilt

- Result class: `disproved estimate`
- Repository status: `DISPROVED`
- Disproved claim: the normalized top-prime-band logarithmic law is uniformly Gaussian on a fixed tilt range containing `theta=1` or `theta=-1`
- Dependencies: N3-ANA-010 and N3-SRC-008

### Statement

At `theta=1`,

\[
\frac{T_{n,1}}{B_{n,1}}\longrightarrow0
\]

in probability. The same holds at `theta=-1` by complement symmetry. Consequently no uniform central limit or Gaussian local-limit theorem can hold on a compact tilt range reaching `|theta|=1`.

### Proof

At `theta=1`, define the minority-bit deficit

\[
D_n=\sum_{p\in\mathcal P_n}(1-A_{p,1})\log p.
\]

The event `E_n` that every bit takes its favored value `A_{p,1}=1` satisfies

\[
\mathbb P(E_n^c)
\le
\sum_{p\in\mathcal P_n}\frac1{1+p}
\le
\frac{2m_n}{n}.
\]

Dusart's upper bound gives

\[
m_n\le\pi(n)\le\frac{n}{\log n-1.1},
\]

hence

\[
\mathbb P(E_n^c)
\le\frac2{\log n-1.1}\to0.
\]

Moreover

\[
\mathbb E D_n
\le
m_n\frac{2\log n}{n}
\le
\frac{2\log n}{\log n-1.1}=O(1).
\]

The same lower-bound argument used above with `theta_0=1` gives

\[
B_{n,1}^2\ge\frac1{48}\log n.
\]

On `E_n`,

\[
T_{n,1}=\mathbb E D_n,
\]

so `T_{n,1}/B_{n,1}->0` there. Since `P(E_n)->1`, convergence to zero in probability follows. A standard normal limit is therefore impossible.

For `theta=-1`, replace every exponent by its complement `1-A_p`.

## Interpretation

The compact condition `theta_0<1` is not cosmetic. On the top prime band it marks the regime in which the total tilted variance grows quickly enough for every coordinate to become negligible. At unit tilt, almost every coordinate freezes at its favored endpoint and Gaussian smoothing fails.

This theorem is a logarithmic-divisor theorem. It must not be transferred to Nova 2's numerical additive characteristic function without a separate matched argument.
