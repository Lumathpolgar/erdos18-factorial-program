## General bounded-exponent model

Fix a cutoff `y=y(n)` and a low-prime exponent vector

\[
\alpha=(a_p)_{p\le y},
\qquad 0\le a_p\le v_p(n!).
\]

For `p>y`, let the independent tilted exponent variables satisfy

\[
\mathbb P_\theta(A_p=j)=
\frac{p^{\theta j}}{1+p^\theta+\cdots+p^{\theta v_p(n!)}},
\qquad 0\le j\le v_p(n!).
\]

Define

\[
T_{n,y,\theta}=
\sum_{y<p\le n}
\bigl(A_p-\mathbb E_\theta A_p\bigr)\log p,
\]

\[
B_{n,y}(\theta)^2=
\operatorname{Var}_\theta(T_{n,y,\theta}).
\]

## Desired fine conclusion

For a frozen compact tilt range, prove in an explicit bulk range

\[
\mathbb P_\theta\{T_{n,y,\theta}\in[x,x+\Delta]\}
=
\frac{\Delta}{\sqrt{2\pi}B_{n,y}(\theta)}
\exp\left(-\frac{x^2}{2B_{n,y}(\theta)^2}\right)
+o\left(\frac{\Delta}{B_{n,y}(\theta)}\right),
\]

uniformly down to the smallest viable `Delta`.

This full fine-window theorem remains open.

## Closed compact-tilt subcase: top prime band

N3-ANA-012 freezes the exact subfamily

\[
\mathcal P_n=\{p:n/2<p\le n\}.
\]

Every coordinate is Bernoulli because `v_p(n!)=1`. For each fixed

\[
0\le\theta_0<1,
\qquad |\theta|\le\theta_0,
\]

and every integer `n>=120368`, it proves

\[
B_{n,\theta}^2
\ge\frac1{48}n^{1-\theta_0}\log n,
\]

and

\[
\sup_z
\left|
\mathbb P_\theta\{T_{n,\theta}/B_{n,\theta}\le z\}
-\Phi(z)
\right|
\le
C_{BE}\sqrt{\frac{48\log n}{n^{1-\theta_0}}}.
\]

For fixed `A>=0`, define

\[
c_A=(2\pi)^{-1/2}e^{-(A+1)^2/2},
\qquad
K_A=4C_{BE}/c_A.
\]

Then

\[
|x|\le AB_{n,\theta},
\qquad
K_A\log n\le\Delta\le B_{n,\theta}
\]

implies

\[
\mathbb P_\theta\{T_{n,\theta}\in[x,x+\Delta]\}
\ge
\frac{c_A}{2}\frac{\Delta}{B_{n,\theta}}.
\]

The theorem also converts this probability into an explicit lower bound for the number of distinct top-prime subset products in the logarithmic interval.

Proof: `proofs/COMPACT_TILT_TOP_PRIME_BAND.md`.

## Sharp compact-range obstruction

N3-ANA-013 proves that at `theta=1` and `theta=-1`,

\[
T_{n,\theta}/B_{n,\theta}\to0
\]

in probability. Therefore a uniform Gaussian theorem cannot extend to a fixed compact tilt interval reaching `|theta|=1` for the top-prime model.

The condition `theta_0<1` is a genuine phase boundary, not a proof artifact.
