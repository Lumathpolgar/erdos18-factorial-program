# Phases 12K through 12P Research Record

These phases tested the remaining lcm-core entry and occupancy architectures after Phase 12J.

## Phase 12K: Entropy-Scale Entry Feasibility Gate

Status: proved obstruction.

A uniform multiplicative mesh at scale

```text
eta_m = exp(-c m / log m)
```

cannot begin near the certified Sylvester-prefix endpoint because the required windows are shorter than integer granularity. The phase also repaired a domain mismatch in the Phase 12J complementary-scale theorem.

Package: `Phase12K_Entropy_Scale_Entry_Feasibility_Gate.zip`

## Phase 12L: Low-Entry Power-Mesh Gate

Status: proposed theorem disproved.

The phase proved the orbit-step ceiling

```text
S_{j+1} <= 2 S_j (D_m(2S_j) + 1)
```

and derived a superlogarithmic lower bound for the maximum-gap greedy orbit. Therefore the greedy route cannot prove the required `O(log m)` local theorem.

Package: `Phase12L_Low_Entry_Power_Mesh_Gate.zip`

## Phase 12M: Non-Greedy Intrinsic-Core Re-entry

Status: capacity survives, separable forms fail.

The phase proved that a legal marked-layer atlas can have enough raw combinatorial profiles. It also proved that independently decoded CRT atlases and separable recursive constructions do not have enough effective capacity for the target.

Package: `Phase12M_Non_Greedy_Intrinsic_Core_Reentry.zip`

## Phase 12N: Additive Window Occupancy Gate

Status: high-prime atlas disproved.

The original magnitude-separated atlas fails at its first post-correction target for an infinite family. A palette-disjoint correction interface repairs that local defect, but the high-prime construction still has an unavoidable additive shell gap.

Package: `Phase12N_Additive_Window_Occupancy_Gate.zip`

## Phase 12O: Mixed-Scale Marker Ladder Gate

Status: finite construction succeeds, asymptotic theorem open at that phase.

A shared-core mixed-scale construction eliminates the earlier quadratic-to-cubic shell gap and passes exact interval-extension certificates through the tested range. The phase explicitly records that this is finite evidence rather than an asymptotic proof.

Package: `Phase12O_Mixed_Scale_Marker_Ladder_Gate.zip`

## Phase 12P: Shared-Core Fiber Flat-Gap Gate

Status: sequential architecture disproved asymptotically.

Smooth-number counting limits the number of useful choices per shared-core layer. A one-choice sequential ladder needs at least

```text
Omega(log m log log m)
```

layers before reaching the necessary range, so it cannot prove an `O(log m)` theorem.

Package: `Phase12P_Shared_Core_Fiber_Flat_Gap_Gate.zip`

## Combined conclusion

The late Track A work does not disprove the lcm theorem or the factorial problem. It proves that the attempted greedy, high-prime, separable, and sequential shared-core mechanisms cannot close the desired asymptotic bound.

The direct factorial program must exploit factorial valuation multiplicity in a way that is not covered by these obstructions, most likely through a globally coupled additive construction or a different direct representation theorem.
